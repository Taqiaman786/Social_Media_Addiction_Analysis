# Social_Media_Addiction_Analysis

📱 Social Media Addiction Analysis — Excel Dashboard
An interactive Excel dashboard analyzing social media addiction patterns among 705 students across multiple countries. The dataset was sourced from Kaggle and transformed into a fully interactive dashboard using Microsoft Excel with Pivot Tables, Slicers, and Charts.

🔍 Key Insights
📌 Platform Usage
•	Instagram is the most used platform — 248 students (35.2%)
•	TikTok ranks second with 154 students (21.8%)
•	Facebook is third with 124 students (17.6%)

📌 Addiction Levels
•	Average addiction score: 6.44 / 10
•	50% of students score 7 or above — indicating moderate to high addiction
•	Average daily social media usage: ~5 hours/day

📌 Academic Impact
•	64.3% of students (453 out of 705) report that social media negatively affects their academic performance

📌 Mental Health
•	Average mental health score: 6.23 / 10
•	Higher daily usage hours correlate with lower mental health scores

📌 Sleep Patterns
•	Average sleep per night: ~6.87 hours among students whose academics are affected
•	Students with lower addiction scores average closer to 7+ hours of sleep

📌 Demographics
•	Near-equal gender split: Female 353 (50.1%) | Male 352 (49.9%)
•	Undergraduates represent the majority at 50.1% of the dataset
•	India, USA, and Canada are the top 3 represented countries

🏁 Conclusion
This analysis of 705 students across 30+ countries reveals a clear and concerning relationship between social media usage and student well-being.
Key takeaways:
•	Instagram and TikTok dominate student screen time, together accounting for over 57% of platform usage.
•	With an average addiction score of 6.44 out of 10, a significant portion of students fall in the moderate-to-high addiction range.
•	64.3% of students acknowledge that social media directly harms their academic performance — making it one of the most critical findings in this dataset.
•	Students with higher usage hours tend to report lower mental health scores and fewer hours of sleep, suggesting a compounding negative effect on overall health.
•	The impact cuts across gender, academic level, and geography, indicating this is a widespread issue rather than an isolated trend.

**Overall, this dashboard highlights the urgent need for digital wellness awareness among students. While social media is an integral part of modern life, mindful usage habits, screen time limits, and institutional support can help students strike a healthier balance between online engagement and academic success.
